• iSearch suggests a simple search engine through which you can easily search uploaded documents from the internet.
  • Use the Search button above in order to begin.
  • Type words you would like to search for, and hit "Enter" or press the "Search" button to the right.

Simple search: Simply type words you're interested to find in a document, separated by "Space" characters
Advanced search: Using the & (AND), | (OR), ! (NOT) Characters, you can further filter your results (! Should be used in combination).

Examples:
  • Software & engineering - Will yield documents containing both software and engineering.
  • Software | Engineering -
    • Will yield documents containing Software
    • Will yield documents containing Engineering
    • Will yield documents containing both software and engineering
  • Software & engineering & !design - Will yield documents containing both software and engineering but not the word design.
* All these special characters can be combined.
Use Soundex button:
  • Using this button you can look for words you are not sure about their spelling.
  • For example the word "fir" will yield documents containing the word "fire".

Admin Panel

As iSearch Admin, you are capable of several operations, some can affect the system dramatically and should be well considered before used:

  • Upload documents: Uploading new documents to the system's database. These documents can then appear in future searches.
    1. Press the "Manage Files" button above.
    2. On the left pane click "Upload Documents".
    3. In the text box, enter the URL(s) of the document(s) you wish to upload.
    4. Press "Upload Documents".
  • View documents: View the system's documents.
    1. Press the "Manage Files" button above.
    2. On the left pane click "View Documents".
  • Disable/Enable documents: In the View Documents page mentioned above, you can click the / button at the right of each document.
    (Notice the Status label changes at the bottom of the document)
    A document with status "Disabled" will not appear in future searches.
